Non of the economics are “right”

  • Ha-joon Chang states that there are many different ways of “conceptualizing and explaining the economy”. This gives rise to very different economic theories and models, non of which is “right”.

18th century

The “invisible hand”

“It is not from the benevolence of the butcher, the brewer, or the baker that we expect our dinner, but from their regard to their own self-interest” - Adam Smith

19th century

Neoclassical economics

  • They argued that the value of a good is determined by the value that consumers place on the good, based on the amount of utility that it brings them, instead of the cost of making the good.
  • Alfred Marshall created the framework of demand and supply (the first demand and supply diagram).
  • Jean-Baptiste Say states that supply creates its own demand.
  • William Stanley Jevons advanced the marginal revolution. (The satisfaction of each additional unit of good - the marginal utility - is less and less).

    Das Kapital!!!

  • Karl Marx pointed to the problems that capitalism creates (Only a lucky minority have the access to the means of production, the unlucky ones are forced to sell their labor).

20th century

Keynesian revolution

  • John Maynard Keynes (father of macroeconomics) stressed the need for government intervention and stabilization policies.

    Monetarist and New Classical counter revolution

  • Milton Friedman strongly opposed ideas of the Keynesian economists. He called for a return to the free market. He believes that the government creates more problems than it solves.

21th century

Behavior economics

  • incorporates elements of psychology into the study of economics.
  • challenges the conventional rational decision-making approach.
  • studies the effect of psychological, cognitive, emotional, cultural and social factors on the economic decisions of individuals and businesses.
