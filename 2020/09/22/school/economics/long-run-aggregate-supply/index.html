Recessionary gap

  • When the actual output level is less than the potential output level a recessionary gap exists
  • At this time unemployment is greater than the natural rate
  • Average prices are falling, thus the situation is also called a deflationary gap
  • Demand for resources and goods is below that required for maximum potential

Inflationary gap

  • When the actual output level is more than the potential output level an inflationary gap exists
  • At this time unemployment is less than the natural rate
  • Average prices are rising
  • Demand for resources and goods is above the potential output level of the economy

Full employment auto-adjustment

Inflationary gap

Adjustment

If AD $\rightarrow$, AS $\leftarrow$.
SRAS shifts inward because resources are scarce.

  • Firms find labor recruitment difficult
  • Firms must compete for adequate supplies of raw materials, office or factory space

    Deflationary gap

Adjustment

If AD $\leftarrow$, AS $\rightarrow$.
SRAS shifts outward because resources are relatively abundant as there is excess capacity.

  • Firms respond to lower demand for goods and services by reducing demand for labor
  • Firms no longer compete for supplies of raw materials, office or factory space
